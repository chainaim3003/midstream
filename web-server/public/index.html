Why batching IS the feature not a latency tax

Per-chunk pricing at $0.0005 is only viable when authorization is off-chain. Gas would otherwise dominate — a $0.01 on-chain cost per authorization would make refusing the next chunk save the buyer less than it costs to operate. So the kill gate would be theatre, not economics. Circle Gateway accepts each signed authorization in milliseconds (off-chain), then batches many of them into one on-chain Arc settlement in the background. The amortization across N actions is what drives per-action overhead toward zero — which is the exact precondition for pay-for-outcomes via mid-stream cutoff. Without batching, sub-cent per-chunk pricing isn't possible, and without sub-cent per-chunk pricing, the kill gate saves nothing.

Per-chunk flow

  • Each paid chunk = one Circle Transfer ID (a UUID, shown in the Payment ledger). The authorization is accepted in milliseconds, off-chain.
  • Buyer's Gateway balance drops immediately (see the Gateway tile in the top bar). Seller is credited immediately. Both are off-chain bookkeeping.
  • Circle batches many UUIDs and settles them as one on-chain Arc tx on its own schedule (empirically every ~15 min during active periods).
  • UUIDs are not Arc tx hashes — they won't load at testnet.arcscan.app/tx/<uuid>.

Where to verify on Arc

  • Resolve button (Payment ledger): shows the UUID's lifecycle status — received → batched → completed. Per Circle's API, the on-chain tx hash is not on the per-transfer record; it lives on the batch.
  • On-chain evidence card (below the live session): every real Arc tx, grouped by Circle's batch settlements. All hashes clickable to testnet.arcscan.app.
  • The buyer's kill decision is instant and free — it never waits for on-chain settlement, because settlement isn't on the critical path.
